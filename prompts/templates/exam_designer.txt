### GOAL

Design a **comprehensive and rigorous exam** that measures both:

1. **Understanding of documented facts and concepts** — comprehension of definitions, hypotheses, experimental setup, and findings.
2. **Ability to apply, reason, and generalize** — transfer of knowledge to related or novel settings, including reasoning about unseen but structurally similar problems.

Your exam should balance factual recall and applied reasoning, ensuring that questions are **non-trivial**, **conceptually coherent**, and **self-contained**.

---

### EXAM DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

#### 1. Identify Key Knowledge Points

* Read the documentation thoroughly.
* List all central ideas, procedures, hypotheses, and findings.
* Identify conceptual dependencies — what must a student understand to apply this knowledge elsewhere.

#### 2. Design Questions

* Create a **diverse set of questions** covering both **factual** and **applied** understanding.
* Vary complexity: include direct factual questions, interpretation tasks, and creative reasoning scenarios.
* Ensure each question aligns with at least one key knowledge point.

#### 3. Example Categories of Question Design

(You are **not limited** to these types — use them as inspiration.)

* **Comprehension & Recall** — identify definitions, parameters, or findings directly stated.
* **Causal & Mechanistic Reasoning** — infer how changes in setup affect results.
* **Hypothetical Transfer** — predict outcomes in analogous or extended tasks.
* **Comparative Analysis** — contrast two methods, hypotheses, or conditions.
* **Experimental Design** — describe how to test, verify, or modify the result in a new domain.
* **Error or Ambiguity Detection** — identify unclear reasoning or assumptions in the documentation.
* **Critical Reflection** — evaluate implications, limitations, or generalization potential of the findings.
* **Creative Synthesis** — propose new questions, variants, or circuits inspired by the documented work.

You may use **multiple-choice**, **free-generation**, or **structured explanation** formats.
Questions must require comprehension, reasoning, or synthesis — not trivial text lookup. No need to test on budgets.

---

### GOLD ANSWERS

For each question, provide a **concise expert reference answer**, based on the documentation or reasoning that logically follows from it.
Each answer must:

* Be unambiguous.
* Reflect the expected understanding of a well-prepared student.
* Reference the relevant section or concept in the documentation when possible.

---

### OUTPUT FORMAT

All exam items must be output as valid **JSON** in the following structure:

```json
{{
    "question_type": "multiple_choice or free_generation",
    "question": "Write the question here.",
    "answer": "Provide the correct or model answer here.",
    "choice": "If it is multiple choice question, you should have your choices here. Provide them in a list", 
    "reference": "Point to relevant section, figure, or concept from documentation."
}}
```

Each question should appear as an element in a JSON array.

All outputs must be saved under the repository path `exam/` ((Please strictly follow my naming and you should output in a exam/ dir under the REPO given to you.):

1. `exam_documentation.ipynb` — notebook containing generated questions and gold answers.
2. `exam_{{task_name}}.json` — JSON file with structured exam data following the format above.

---

### QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

* Questions must be **clear**, **non-trivial**, and **grounded in documentation**.
* The exam should **span the full range** of knowledge in the document: conceptual, procedural, and analytical.
* Applied questions should challenge reasoning and creativity, not memorization.
* Answers must be **accurate**, **justified**, and **internally consistent**.
* Include at least one question per major section or concept cluster.
* The overall exam should balance:

  * 40–60% factual understanding
  * 40–60% reasoning and application
